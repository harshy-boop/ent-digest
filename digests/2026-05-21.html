Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis

Benefits of bilateral cochlear implantation in children: a systematic review and meta-analysis.

Zhuchkova A, Remacha J, Sandoval M, Codina A et al. … Bernal-Sprekelsen M (Faculty of Medicine, Departament d´Especialitats Quirùrgiques, Hospital Clinic, University of Barcelona, c/Casanova 143,)

BACKGROUND

The benefit of bilateral cochlear implantation (BiCI) over unilateral (UniCI) in children has been demonstrated in individual studies, but pooled effect sizes to guide counselling were lacking. This PROSPERO-registered systematic review synthesised evidence from 2015 through June 2025.

METHODS

PRISMA-compliant SR+MA across PubMed, ScienceDirect, Scopus, and Cochrane. Eligible studies: children with congenital/early-onset bilateral deafness receiving BiCI or UniCI. Random-effects meta-analyses using standardised mean differences (SMD). 50 studies in qualitative synthesis; 20 contributing to meta-analyses.

RESULTS

BiCI significantly outperformed UniCI in speech perception in noise (SMD=+0.70, p<0.001), sound localisation (SMD=+0.74, p<0.001), and receptive vocabulary/language development (SMD=+0.65). QoL consistently higher in BiCI users. Surgical/vestibular complications were infrequent and did not exceed those of UniCI. Language benefits showed greater heterogeneity than auditory outcomes.

LIMITATIONS

Methodological and clinical heterogeneity across studies; results for language outcomes less consistent than for auditory measures; most studies non-randomised due to ethical constraints on withholding BiCI. Need for standardised longitudinal assessment tools.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

Bilateral cochlear implantation is safe and confers meaningful auditory and language advantages in children over unilateral implantation. Benefits are most robust with early (ideally simultaneous) surgery. CI teams should counsel families that BiCI is the preferred strategy when feasible, with the caveat that language heterogeneity warrants ongoing follow-up.

Why score 4: PRISMA-compliant systematic review and meta-analysis (50 studies, 20 contributing to quantitative synthesis) in European Archives of ORL (specialty flagship). Bilateral CI shows significant advantages over unilateral: speech in noise SMD=0.70, sound localisation SMD=0.74, language development SMD=0.65 — all p<0.001. Benefits most robust with early, simultaneous implantation. Safety profile comparable to unilateral CI.

Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis

Predictors of thyroid cancer in paediatric patients with thyroid nodules: A systematic review and meta-analysis.

Shah S, Walters B, Balasubramanian SP, Edafe O … Edafe O (ENT Department, University Hospital Lewisham, Sheffield, SE13 6LH, UK.)

BACKGROUND

Paediatric thyroid nodules carry a higher malignancy rate than adult nodules (up to 30%), yet risk stratification tools have not been validated in this population. This SR+MA characterised predictors of thyroid cancer in children with thyroid nodules.

METHODS

PubMed, Embase, and Cochrane searched through April 6, 2026. Inclusion: paediatric patients with thyroid nodules, with histological confirmation. Random-effects model. Quality assessment via Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (majority scored ≥7 stars). 16 studies included, 2,634 patients (794 malignant, 1,840 benign).

RESULTS

Independent predictors of malignancy: intranodal calcifications OR 10.33 (95% CI 3.20–33.37), palpable lymphadenopathy OR 7.73 (3.03–19.75), increased vascularity OR 3.21 (1.95–5.28), prior radiation exposure OR 1.96 (1.15–3.34), and elevated TSH (mean difference +0.92 mIU/L). Papillary thyroid cancer accounted for 84.5% of malignant cases.

LIMITATIONS

Heterogeneity in imaging criteria and cytological thresholds across studies; mostly retrospective cohorts; limited data on molecular markers. Geographic and healthcare-system variation may affect generalisability.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

In children with thyroid nodules, calcifications and lymphadenopathy are the strongest red flags for malignancy — either should prompt expedited cytological workup (FNA) and surgical referral. Elevated TSH and increased vascularity on ultrasound provide additional incremental risk stratification beyond size alone.

Why score 4: Systematic review and meta-analysis (16 studies, 2,634 patients) in European Archives of ORL (specialty flagship). Provides the first pooled quantification of malignancy-risk predictors in paediatric thyroid nodules — directly useful for pre-operative workup decisions by ENT surgeons and endocrinologists.

Network Meta-Analysis

Efficacy and safety of biologics for eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis: a network meta-analysis.

Zhang Y, Yang Y … Yang Y (Department of Otolaryngology, Beijing Hospital, National Center of Gerontology, National Clinical Research Center for Ge)

BACKGROUND

Eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (EGPA) — formerly Churg-Strauss syndrome — commonly manifests with sinonasal polyposis, rhinitis, and asthma alongside systemic vasculitis. Multiple biologics are now available but no head-to-head RCTs exist. This NMA provides indirect comparisons.

METHODS

Systematic search (PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials.gov through October 2025). Network meta-analysis, random-effects model, PRISMA guidelines. 17 articles, 1,150 adult EGPA participants. Four biologics compared: mepolizumab (100 mg, 300 mg), benralizumab, rituximab, omalizumab. Primary outcome: MIRRA-defined remission rate. Secondary: OCS dose reduction, relapse rate, adverse events.

RESULTS

Versus placebo/conventional therapy: benralizumab OR=6.18 (95% CI 1.98–19.31) and mepolizumab 300mg OR=5.83 (2.00–17.02) showed strongest remission rates. Mepolizumab 100mg OR=3.70 (1.26–10.92). Both benralizumab and mepolizumab 300mg reduced OCS dosage and prevented asthma/vasculitis relapse. Overall and serious AE rates were comparable to placebo across all biologics.

LIMITATIONS

NMA relies on indirect treatment comparisons; included studies varied in EGPA phase (relapsing vs refractory vs new-onset); rituximab and omalizumab had fewer studies contributing. OCS definitions varied across trials.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

When choosing a biologic for EGPA, benralizumab and mepolizumab 300mg are the most evidence-supported options for achieving remission and reducing oral corticosteroid burden. ENT surgeons co-managing EGPA patients with nasal polyposis should advocate for these agents over rituximab or omalizumab in the vasculitis context. Safety profiles are reassuring across all four biologics.

Why score 4: Network meta-analysis (17 articles, 1,150 participants, 4 biologics: mepolizumab, benralizumab, rituximab, omalizumab) with PRISMA methodology. EGPA is an ENT-relevant disease (sinonasal polyposis, rhinitis, airway eosinophilia). First head-to-head NMA comparing all available biologics: benralizumab (OR=6.18) and mepolizumab 300mg (OR=5.83) emerge as preferred agents. Direct practice guidance on biologic choice.

Oncologic, functional, and economic outcomes of transoral robotic surgery for HPV-associated oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma: a systematic review and meta-analysis of pathology and treatment strategies.

Meta-analysis of 22 studies evaluating TORS for HPV-positive oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma. Pooled 2-year OS 94%, 3-year OS 96%, 5-year OS 92%. Functional outcomes favourable: postoperative haemorrhage 7%, tracheostomy rate 9%, long-term gastrostomy dependence only 2%. In salvage setting, 2-year OS 76%. Results support TORS as an effective first-line option in selected HPV+ OPSCC patients with manageable complication rates, though heterogeneity in study design limits definitive conclusions about treatment de-escalation.

Clinical Application of Voice Fatigue Testing in Treating Voice Fatigue Among Primary and Secondary School Teachers.

Randomised controlled trial (n=60 teachers) comparing voice training (n=30) vs vocal rest (n=30) for vocal fatigue, using the vocal loading task (VLT) as both treatment-monitoring and outcome tool. Annual Versus Patient-Initiated Follow-Up in Sleep Apnoea Care: A Quasi-Experimental Study.

Quasi-experimental comparison of two CPAP follow-up models (scheduled annual vs patient-initiated) across two sleep clinics (Denmark). After 2 years, CPAP adherence was 80% in both groups. First study to compare these models: suggests patient-initiated, virtual CPAP follow-up is a viable alternative that may improve healthcare resource efficiency.

Interaural Asymmetry, Not Hearing Thresholds, Predicts Diotic Frequency Modulation Sensitivity in Children With Minimal-to-Mild Hearing Loss.

Case-control study (Ear and Hearing journal) examining diotic frequency modulation (FM) sensitivity in children with minimal-to-mild hearing loss (MMHL). Interaural asymmetry — not absolute hearing threshold — was the primary predictor of FM sensitivity deficits. Children with symmetric MMHL performed differently from those with asymmetric MMHL, suggesting that asymmetry drives central auditory processing changes even at mild loss levels. Clinical implication: audiological assessment of children with MMHL should include binaural processing tests, not just threshold measures.

Clinical outcomes in older adults with advanced thyroid cancer.

Retrospective review (n=114) of patients ≥65 with advanced thyroid cancer receiving systemic therapy at Princess Margaret Cancer Centre (2010–2025). 65/114 received first-line systemic therapy. Older adults experienced comparable objective response rates but higher rates of dose reductions/discontinuations due to toxicity. Overall survival outcomes were shorter in older cohort vs historic younger comparators. Reinforces need for careful toxicity monitoring and dose individualisation when prescribing VEGFR inhibitors or other systemic agents in older thyroid cancer patients.
