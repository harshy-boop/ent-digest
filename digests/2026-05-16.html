Concordance Between an Adaptive Sino-Nasal Outcome Test and Imaging Results in Chronic Rhinosinusitis.

Zhang SK, Prince AA, Corrales CE et al. … Shin JJ (senior, Department of Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery, Mass General Brigham, Boston, Massachusetts.)

BACKGROUND

CRS is assessed globally with SNOT-22, yet known discordance exists between SNOT-22 scores and objective CT (Lund-Mackay) findings. A novel adaptive SNOT instrument applies item-response theory to dynamically select questions.

METHODS

Consecutive patients at tertiary and community sites in Massachusetts (May 2020–March 2025). Factor analysis included 3,661 respondents; 417 had CT imaging. Ordinal regression compared static SNOT-22 and adaptive SNOT nasal-domain scores with Lund-Mackay CT scores.

RESULTS

Static SNOT-22 (all versions) showed no measurable association with LMCT. The adaptive SNOT nasal domain was significantly associated with LMCT (OR 3.75; 95% CI 1.90–7.40). Adaptive allergy testing was inversely associated with LMCT (OR 0.47), allowing differentiation of CRS from allergic rhinitis.

LIMITATIONS

Single-geographic cohort; adaptive instrument requires prospective external validation in non-specialist settings. Study design is observational.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

SNOT-22, the current standard, does not correlate with CT disease burden. The adaptive SNOT closes this gap and can distinguish CRS from allergic rhinitis — a meaningful clinical advance for both practice and research enrollment.

Why score 4: Large prospective diagnostic study (n=3,661 factor analysis; 417 with imaging) in JAMA Otolaryngology from Mass General Brigham. Shows that static SNOT-22 is NOT associated with CT imaging findings, while the novel adaptive SNOT IS — directly challenging the tool used worldwide to assess CRS.

Vitamin D Supplementation for Preventing Recurrent Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo: A Randomized Clinical Study.

Tawfeeq HA, Zaidan MA, Ghanim AK … Ghanim AK (senior, Al-Karkh Health Directorate, Ministry of Health, Baghdad, Iraq.)

BACKGROUND

BPPV recurs in 10–50% of patients. Vitamin D deficiency has been proposed as a modifiable risk factor; prior data are inconsistent and mostly uncontrolled.

METHODS

160 adults with confirmed idiopathic BPPV and 25-OH vitamin D <20 ng/mL were randomized 1:1 to cholecalciferol 10,000 IU weekly vs. matched placebo for 24 months. Primary outcome: BPPV recurrence (ITT analysis with multiple imputation).

RESULTS

Cholecalciferol significantly raised serum 25-OH-D (24 mo: 24.8 vs. 9.8 ng/mL; p<0.001). Cumulative BPPV recurrence was significantly lower in the treatment arm at all time points (6, 12, 18, and 24 months). Safety profile was excellent.

LIMITATIONS

Single-country (Iraq) tertiary cohort limits generalizability. Non-specific "cholecalciferol" regimen; optimal dosing not established. No blinding confirmation via serum levels mid-trial.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

Screen vitamin D-deficient BPPV patients. If 25-OH-D <20 ng/mL, cholecalciferol supplementation is a low-cost, low-risk adjunct that meaningfully reduces recurrence over 24 months.

Why score 4: Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT (n=160, 24-month follow-up) in the Annals of Otology. Vitamin D supplementation significantly reduced BPPV recurrence at every time point. BPPV is the most common vestibular disorder worldwide; a simple, safe intervention with strong effect size is practice-ready.

Rethinking prophylactic central neck dissection in clinically node-negative papillary thyroid cancer.

Hartl DM, Schlumberger M … Schlumberger M (senior, Department of Nuclear Medicine and Endocrine Oncology, Gustave Roussy, Villejuif, France.)

BACKGROUND

Papillary thyroid cancer (PTC) has a high rate of occult central-compartment lymph node metastases. Whether to remove these prophylactically (pCND) in clinically node-negative (cN0) patients remains controversial because clinical recurrence occurs in <10% even without pCND.

METHODS

Narrative review and personal view synthesising clinical trial data, guideline history, and outcomes research. Authors are head of thyroid surgery and nuclear medicine at Gustave Roussy.

RESULTS

No randomised trial has shown a survival advantage from pCND. Some studies show marginal reduction in regional recurrence, but at the cost of increased temporary hypoparathyroidism (up to 30%). Most guidelines leave the decision open; true equipoise remains.

LIMITATIONS

No new primary data; RCTs in surgical oncology are methodologically challenging. Authors acknowledge publication bias favours centres performing pCND routinely.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

In cN0 PTC, prophylactic CND adds morbidity without proven survival benefit. The Lancet platform signals this view is gaining traction. Clinicians should be prepared to defend selective (omission) strategies to patients and institutions.

Why score 4: Personal View in Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology by Hartl and Schlumberger (Gustave Roussy), the leading thyroid surgical group in Europe. Synthesises the ongoing controversy over prophylactic central neck dissection in cN0 PTC and proposes a research agenda. Lancet source + key opinion leaders on a topic still without guideline consensus earns score 4.

Impact of Molecular Testing on Surgical Decision-Making in Indeterminate Thyroid Nodules: A Global Meta-Analysis Across Test Generations.

Nguyen TP, Bychkov A, Jung CK et al. … Rana C (senior, Department of Pathology, King George's Medical University, Lucknow, India. chanchalrana@kgmcindia.ed)

BACKGROUND

Bethesda III/IV thyroid nodules have uncertain malignancy risk and frequently lead to diagnostic hemithyroidectomy. Molecular platforms (Afirma, ThyroSeq, ThyGeNEXT) are marketed to reduce unnecessary surgery, but comparative cross-platform data are sparse.

METHODS

PRISMA-guided systematic review and meta-analysis. 132 studies, 66,448 nodules (45.6% indeterminate). Primary outcomes: surgical resection rate and risk of malignancy among resected nodules, stratified by molecular testing vs. conventional management and by test generation.

RESULTS

Conventional management had 2.3× higher surgical rates than molecular testing (OR 2.26; 95% CI 1.55–3.29; p<0.001). Second-generation tests further reduced surgical rates vs. first-generation (OR 1.71; p=0.013). Molecular testing did not compromise detection of clinically significant malignancy.

LIMITATIONS

High study heterogeneity; many included studies are observational. NIFTP reclassification affects malignancy rates across eras. Cost-effectiveness not assessed.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

Molecular testing for Bethesda III/IV nodules reduces surgery by half without compromising cancer detection, particularly with second-generation platforms. Consider uptake in practices where repeat FNA or diagnostic hemithyroidectomy is the current default.

Why score 4: PRISMA-guided meta-analysis of 132 studies (66,448 nodules; 30,292 indeterminate) across molecular test generations. Shows molecular testing reduces surgery by >2× and second-generation platforms further improve selection. Directly applicable to thyroid nodule management decisions.

Multi-Frequency Electrocochleography Results in Fewer Drop Alarms During Cochlear Implant Insertion.

Espahbodi M, Syed A, Tu A et al. … Harris MS (senior, Department of Otolaryngology & Communication Sciences, Medical College of Wisconsin, Milwaukee, Wisc)

BACKGROUND

Electrocochleography (ECochG) during cochlear implant insertion guides surgeons in real time: an amplitude drop signals possible intracochlear damage. Single-frequency algorithms generate frequent false alarms, potentially disrupting insertion and eroding confidence in the signal.

METHODS

Multi-institutional prospective cohort at 18 high-volume CI centres, 2024–2025. Adults with SNHL (≤90 dB HL at 500 Hz) receiving Advanced Bionics Ultra 3D implants. Simultaneous multi-frequency ECochG (125–4000 Hz) vs. historical single-frequency approach; drop alarm defined as ≥6 dB amplitude decrease.

RESULTS

Mean drop alarms: single-frequency 1.72 vs. multi-frequency 0.42 (p<0.001). Type C patterns (rise then drop) and Type D (no-response) decreased with multi-frequency, indicating broader cochlear coverage and fewer spurious alarms.

LIMITATIONS

Single implant system (Advanced Bionics Ultra 3D); long-term hearing preservation outcomes not yet reported. Post-hoc design for multi-frequency comparison.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

Surgeons using ECochG-guided CI insertion should be aware that multi-frequency algorithms substantially reduce false drop alarms, allowing more reliable intraoperative monitoring. Expect this to become the standard approach as the technology is adopted.

Why score 4: 18-centre prospective cohort (195 ears) in The Laryngoscope from Medical College of Wisconsin. Novel multi-frequency ECochG algorithm reduces drop alarms during CI insertion ~4-fold (1.72 → 0.42; p<0.001). Intraoperative hearing-preservation monitoring directly affects daily CI practice.
