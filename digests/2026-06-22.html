Endoscopic Sinus Surgery for Recurrent Acute Rhinosinusitis: A Randomized Clinical Trial

Kaski H, Alakärppä A, Laajala A, Tokola P … Alho OP (senior, Oulu University Hospital, Finland)

BACKGROUND

Recurrent acute rhinosinusitis (RARS — ≥3 episodes/6 months or ≥4/year) dramatically impairs quality of life, yet evidence for endoscopic sinus surgery (ESS) in this indication has been sparse relative to chronic rhinosinusitis.

METHODS

Open-label RCT at Oulu University Hospital (Finland), May 2020–December 2024. Adults with physician-confirmed RARS and insufficient symptom control on conservative therapy randomized 1:1 to ESS + medical therapy vs. medical therapy alone. Primary outcome: SNOT-22 change at 6 months. ITT analysis; n=59 (30 operative, 29 conservative).

RESULTS

Mean SNOT-22 improvement was 22.4 points greater in the ESS group (95% CI, 13.7–31.1). This exceeds the minimal clinically important difference (~9 points). ESS patients also had fewer healthcare visits, fewer antibiotic courses, and fewer work absences. Only 4 operative complications (3 infections, 1 synechia), no major adverse events.

LIMITATIONS

Small sample (n=59); open-label design (no sham control); single Finnish center with limited generalizability; 6-month follow-up only. Predominantly female population (88%) noted.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

This is the first RCT to demonstrate that ESS provides a clinically meaningful, statistically significant benefit over medical therapy alone in adults with RARS. Consider broadening the ESS conversation to RARS patients who have failed conservative management — this trial directly supports that discussion.

Why score 5: RCT in JAMA Otolaryngol, the top ENT subspecialty journal. Effect size (22.4 SNOT-22 points) is clinically meaningful and exceeded all secondary endpoints. Addresses a longstanding evidence gap in a common clinical scenario. Despite modest n and open-label design, this is the best prospective evidence available for ESS in RARS and will likely influence next-cycle guidelines.

Effectiveness of Over-the-Counter Hearing Aids Versus Professionally Fitted Devices: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis

De Sousa KC, Almufarrij I, Kruger M, Manchaiah V … Swanepoel W (senior, University of Pretoria, South Africa)

BACKGROUND

Since FDA over-the-counter (OTC) hearing aid regulations took effect in 2022, patients are buying self-fitting OTC devices without audiology assessment. Clinicians need data on whether outcomes differ versus professionally fitted aids.

METHODS

SR/meta-analysis of 15 unique field trials (24 records) comparing FDA-cleared self-fitting OTC aids with audiologist-fitted prescription devices in adults. Databases searched through August 2025; 774 participants, 739 analyzed. Random-effects meta-analysis with Hedges' g; GRADE certainty assessment.

RESULTS

No significant differences detected across four key patient-reported or objective outcomes: APHAB (g=−0.05), SSQ-12 (g=0.01), IOI-HA (g=0.12), QuickSIN (g=0.03). Confidence intervals were narrow for functional outcomes. Risk of bias moderate-high; GRADE certainty: low.

LIMITATIONS

Most trials were short-term (10 days–8 weeks), not representative of real-world sustained use; many compared self-fit and professional fit of the same device (not OTC vs. premium aids); GRADE certainty is low.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

Self-fitting OTC hearing aids appear to deliver comparable short-term benefit to professionally fitted devices. For counseling mildly-to-moderately hearing impaired adults who inquire about OTC options: these data support them as a valid starting point, while complex audiologic needs, asymmetric loss, or prior OTC failure warrant professional evaluation.

Why score 4: Published in OTO-HNS flagship, directly addresses a practice-level question every audiologist and otologist faces since OTC legalization. Meta-analysis design with 774 participants adds weight. Key limitation (low GRADE certainty, short studies) appropriately tempers enthusiasm but doesn't diminish the clinical relevance.

Dupilumab Extended Interval Dosing in Chronic Rhinosinusitis With Nasal Polyps: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis

Alqutub A, Herzallah AI, Alqutub S, Assiri SA … Herzallah IR (senior, King Abdullah Medical City, Makkah, Saudi Arabia)

BACKGROUND

Dupilumab 300 mg Q2W is FDA-approved for CRSwNP. Real-world clinicians have been extending to Q4W or longer in stable patients to reduce injection burden and cost. No controlled trial has tested this strategy — only observational data exist.

METHODS

Systematic review with random-effects meta-analysis. 19 studies met inclusion, 9 contributed quantitative data (n=1075 patients) who transitioned from Q2W to extended intervals (≥Q4W) after 6–12 months induction. Outcomes: SNOT-22, nasal congestion, olfaction, NPS, Lund-Mackay CT score, eosinophil counts.

RESULTS

All outcomes showed non-significant changes after extending to Q4W: SNOT-22 (−0.56), nasal polyp score (−0.29), Lund-Mackay score (−1.25), olfaction (+0.23). Eosinophils decreased significantly, suggesting ongoing biologic activity. Subgroup analysis: 12 months of induction before spacing appeared safer than 6 months.

LIMITATIONS

All included studies are observational (no controlled arm); heterogeneity in extension intervals and induction periods; mostly high-income center data; no long-term relapse data beyond the interval extension period.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

In stable CRSwNP patients after ≥12 months of standard Q2W dosing, extending dupilumab to Q4W appears to maintain disease control based on current data. This has meaningful cost and convenience implications. A formal tapering protocol remains to be validated, and individualized patient selection (biomarker profile, prior polyp burden) is warranted before spacing.

Why score 4: Meta-analysis of 1075 patients in OTO-HNS flagship. Directly informs a common clinical question: can I extend the dosing interval in a biologic-stabilized CRSwNP patient? Observational nature of underlying studies limits certainty, but the pooled findings are consistent across outcomes. High practice-relevance given biologic cost and prior authorization burden.

Transoral Ultrasound in the Diagnostic Workup of Peritonsillar Abscess — A Randomized Clinical Trial

Todsen T, Stage MG, Melchiors J, Mejdahl M … Michaelsen SH (senior, Odense University Hospital, Denmark)

BACKGROUND

Distinguishing peritonsillar abscess (PTA) from cellulitis on clinical exam is difficult, leading to unnecessary blind needle aspirations. Transoral ultrasound (TOUS) has been proposed as a point-of-care tool but lacked RCT evidence.

METHODS

Multicenter RCT across two Danish university ENT departments. Adults with suspected PTA randomized to standard clinical exam alone (n=48) vs. exam + TOUS (n=47). Primary outcome: diagnostic accuracy. Secondary: number of aspiration attempts. 28 different physicians performed evaluations.

RESULTS

Diagnostic accuracy: 80.9% (TOUS group) vs. 72.9% (control); p=0.50 — no significant difference. However, unsuccessful needle aspiration attempts: 26.0% vs. 37.5%, and the TOUS group required 31.9% fewer aspiration attempts overall (p<0.01). No significant difference in patient outcomes.

LIMITATIONS

Multicenter but limited to two Danish centers; diverse operator experience (28 physicians) may affect accuracy; study may be underpowered to detect diagnostic accuracy differences. No long-term follow-up.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

Adding TOUS to the PTA workup significantly reduces unnecessary needle aspiration attempts (~32% fewer), though it did not improve diagnostic accuracy or final outcomes in this trial. For departments with access to point-of-care ultrasound, incorporating TOUS can spare patients failed aspiration attempts. It does not replace clinical judgment but adds procedural efficiency.

Why score 4: Multicenter RCT published in Laryngoscope. The reduction in unnecessary aspiration attempts (31.9%, p<0.01) is a meaningful, patient-centered finding even without a diagnostic accuracy gain. PTA is one of the highest-volume ENT emergencies, making this operationally relevant. Clean RCT design with diverse operators increases generalizability.

Artificial Intelligence-Based Risk Prediction Models for Complications After Tongue Cancer Surgery

Matar DY, Matar AY, Nimbalkar A, Saad CG … Panayi AC (senior, University Hospital Zurich, Switzerland)

BACKGROUND

Glossectomy for tongue cancer carries substantial postoperative morbidity. Current generic risk tools (ACS-NSQIP calculator) lack individualized prediction for tongue cancer-specific complications such as bleeding requiring transfusion.

METHODS

Retrospective ACS-NSQIP data, 2008–2024, >700 US hospitals. 8,266 adults undergoing glossectomy for malignant tongue tumors. Six ML models trained on 31 preoperative variables; validated on 2024 data. Outcomes: 6 major 30-day events including reoperation, SSI, pneumonia, bleeding, and composite complications.

RESULTS

XGBoost and LightGBM models performed comparably to ACS-NSQIP calculator for most outcomes and provided new prediction for bleeding (AUROC 0.88–0.90), not previously available. Resulted in PRO-TONGUE — a publicly available, interpretable, outcome-specific online risk calculator for preoperative counseling.

LIMITATIONS

Retrospective NSQIP data; 30-day outcomes only; no external validation outside NSQIP hospitals; model requires prospective real-world validation before widespread clinical adoption.

CLINICAL TAKEAWAY

PRO-TONGUE (publicly available) offers surgeons an individualized, interpretable risk estimate for 6 major 30-day complications before tongue cancer surgery, including bleeding prediction not currently in ACS-NSQIP. External validation is still needed, but the tool is available now and may be useful for preoperative patient counseling and shared decision-making.

Why score 4: JAMA Otolaryngol publication, large NSQIP cohort (8,266 cases), directly produces a publicly available clinical tool. Although retrospective and needing external validation, the sample size, multi-outcome scope, and journal tier justify a score-4. The bleeding prediction gap filled here is practically useful for head-and-neck surgeons.
