Lateral Wall Versus Perimodiolar Electrodes for Hearing Preservation in Cochlear Implantation: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.

Eshraghi A et al. … Eshraghi AA (senior, Dept. of Otolaryngology & Cochlear Implant Lab, University of Miami)

Background

Hearing preservation (HP) during cochlear implantation enables acoustic + electric stimulation, improving QoL. Whether electrode design—lateral wall (LW) vs perimodiolar (PM)—affects HP rates has been debated.

Methods

PRISMA systematic review + random-effects meta-analysis (Jan 2018–Apr 2025). 76 studies screened; 72 (n=3,783 ears) included. JBI bias assessment; subgroup analysis by follow-up; I² heterogeneity.

Results

Pooled HP: LW 45.9% vs PM 34.2% (P<0.000005). Head-to-head (18 studies, n=1,710): RR 1.24, P=0.057. Sensitivity analysis (excl. ≤1 mo f/u): RR 1.37 [1.06–1.77], P=0.016—significant LW superiority.

Limitations

HP definitions vary; mostly retrospective. Head-to-head RR only significant in sensitivity analysis. Cochlear anatomy and surgical technique are uncontrolled.

Clinical Takeaway

Lateral wall arrays should be the default when HP is the surgical priority. Robust enough to influence institutional protocols and informed-consent discussions at CI programs.

Why score 5: 72-study PRISMA meta-analysis of 3,783 ears in the specialty flagship journal. LW superiority confirmed by sensitivity analysis (RR 1.37; P=0.016). Directly changes surgical electrode selection.

Antiviral Prophylaxis for Delayed Facial Nerve Palsy Following Vestibular Schwannoma Resection: Single Institutional Series With Systematic Literature Review and Meta-Analyses of 7136 Patients.

Youssef AS et al. … Youssef AS (senior, Dept. of Neurosurgery, University of Colorado School of Medicine, Anschutz Medical Campus)

Background

Delayed facial nerve palsy (DFNP, worsening ≥2 HB grades on PODs 5–30) affects ~12% of VS resections. Viral reactivation (HSV/VZV) implicated; antiviral prophylaxis inconsistently used.

Methods

Two parts: (1) institutional cohort of 301 VS resections pre/post valacyclovir 1000 mg TID × 7 days; (2) PRISMA meta-analysis of 28 studies (n=6,835) combined = 7,136 total.

Results

Institutional DFNP 5%; no significant difference with/without valacyclovir (P=0.42). Background DFNP incidence 12.8%. Meta-analysis (5 comparative studies): antiviral prophylaxis reduced DFNP (RR 0.73 [0.60–0.88]; NNT=16). >80% recovered HB I–II regardless.

Limitations

No RCT; only 5 comparative studies; institutional cohort underpowered. High natural recovery rate complicates benefit attribution.

Clinical Takeaway

Pooled data (7,136 patients) support perioperative valacyclovir prophylaxis (NNT=16, minimal cost/risk). Skull base programs not currently using this should consider adopting while awaiting RCT.

Why score 5: Largest pooled analysis on VS-related delayed facial palsy (7,136 patients). NNT=16 with low-risk drug. Practice-adoptable finding for skull base programs.

American Thyroid Association 2026 Guidelines for Thyroid Disease in Preconception, Pregnancy, and Postpartum.

ATA Task Force, Shan Z et al.

Background

Thyroid disease is the second most common endocrine disorder in pregnancy. Prior ATA guideline from 2017; substantial new evidence on TSH targets, Graves' disease biologics, and cancer in pregnancy.

Methods

Multinational 10-society task force + patient advocates. GRADE-based systematic review. Good Practice Statements where RCT data absent. Informed by stakeholder feedback and global needs assessment.

Results

Eight domains updated: thyroid function testing; iodine supplementation; autoimmunity; hypothyroidism (revised TSH targets); isolated hypothyroxinemia; Graves' disease (new antithyroid drug guidance); nodules/cancer in pregnancy; postpartum thyroid dysfunction.

Limitations

Much evidence low-to-moderate quality. TSH reference ranges vary by assay/trimester. Universal screening remains controversial.

Clinical Takeaway

All clinicians managing thyroid disease in reproductive-age women must review this 2026 guideline now. It supersedes 2017 ATA, updates TSH thresholds, Graves' disease protocols, and nodule surveillance in pregnancy.

Why score 5: Major new ATA CPG replacing the 2017 edition. GRADE methodology, multinational authorship. Mandatory reading for thyroid surgeons, endocrinologists, and obstetricians.

Meta-Analysisn=98,604

Recurrence Risk of Differentiated Thyroid Cancer Treated with Total or Hemithyroidectomy. Systematic Review and Meta-analysis.

Trimboli P et al. … Trimboli P (senior, Università della Svizzera Italiana, Lugano)

Background

Hemithyroidectomy (HT) increasingly used for low-risk DTC. Direct comparisons with total thyroidectomy (TT) recurrence have been inconsistent.

Methods

PRISMA review of PubMed/Embase through May 2025. 46 retrospective studies, 98,604 patients (TT n=75,531; HT n=23,073). Primary: risk difference in recurrence. Random-effects model.

Results

Recurrence: TT 5.8% vs HT 8.1%. HT higher risk (OR 1.56 [1.47–1.66]; I²=85%). Absolute RD: +3% for HT. Consistent in PTC subgroup (OR 1.87).

Limitations

All retrospective; selection bias (lower-risk tumors get HT). High heterogeneity (I²=85–87%). Long-term disease-specific survival not captured.

Clinical Takeaway

When counseling DTC patients on surgical extent, incorporate a 3% absolute excess recurrence risk with HT. Consistent across 98,604 patients. Should be explicitly discussed in shared decision-making.

Why score 4: Largest meta-analysis on surgical extent in DTC (98,604 patients). Consistent OR 1.56 signal for recurrence with HT. High heterogeneity limits certainty but magnitude is relevant for surgical counseling.

Meta-Analysisn=411R/M HNSCC

Efficacy of cetuximab-based regimens after immune checkpoint inhibitor failure in recurrent or metastatic head and neck squamous cell carcinoma: a systematic review and meta-analysis.

Yosefof E et al. … Yosefof E (senior, Dept. of Otolaryngology H&N Surgery, Rabin Medical Center / Tel Aviv University)

Background

After ICI failure in R/M HNSCC (pembrolizumab-based first-line), no established standard of care exists. Cetuximab widely used second-line but post-ICI efficacy was unknown.

Methods

PRISMA random-effects meta-analysis; 4 databases (Jan 2014–Jan 2026). Outcomes: ORR, DCR, PFS, OS. 14 studies, 411 patients.

Results

Pooled ORR: 47% (37–56%). DCR: 70% (58–80%). Median PFS: 4.69 mo. Median OS: 10.71 mo. Cetuximab+chemo numerically higher ORR vs monotherapy (not significant).

Limitations

All retrospective; no prospective RCT. Selection bias. Non-uniform response definitions. Cannot guide biomarker selection.

Clinical Takeaway

Cetuximab shows meaningful activity post-ICI in R/M HNSCC (ORR ~47%). Best available evidence for tumor board discussions while prospective trials are awaited.

Why score 4: First meta-analysis on second-line therapy after ICI failure in R/M HNSCC—no established standard exists. ORR 47% and OS 10.71 mo directly inform tumor board discussions.
